# Patch App Landing Page Project Brief

## Commercial Strategy

Patch App is transitioning from a manual onboarding model to an app-first model. The new landing page should support that commercial shift by making the app feel like the primary product experience, not an afterthought behind a service-led onboarding flow.

The page should position Patch as a premium, modern legal technology company for lawyers and international legal professionals. It should borrow the clarity, confidence, and app-first feel of companies such as Uber, Bolt, Grab, and Glovo while adapting the tone and visuals for a more professional legal audience.

The site should eventually replace the current Unbounce landing page, but only after the new AWS Amplify-hosted version is built, tested, connected to the domain, and verified.

## Page Objective

The primary objective of the landing page is to move qualified visitors toward downloading the Patch app.

The page should quickly answer:

- What Patch is.
- Who Patch is for.
- Why it is useful to legal professionals.
- Why the app is the best way to start.
- What action the visitor should take next.

The page should support a premium first impression and reduce reliance on manual onboarding as the default path.

## Target Audience

The primary audience is lawyers and international legal professionals who need a reliable, polished, and efficient way to engage with Patch through the app.

Likely audience groups include:

- Lawyers working across borders.
- International legal professionals.
- Professionals handling clients, matters, referrals, documents, or legal coordination across jurisdictions.
- Early adopters who value modern mobile-first professional tools.

The tone should be confident, direct, and professional. It should feel modern and app-led without becoming casual in a way that weakens trust.

## Design Direction

The future landing page should feel like a premium app-first company website.

Design inspiration:

- Uber: direct, confident, product-led.
- Bolt: clean, fast, accessible.
- Grab: practical, app-first, service ecosystem feel.
- Glovo: high-conversion consumer app flow, adapted carefully for a professional context.

Design principles:

- Lead with the Patch App as the main product.
- Make the app download action prominent.
- Use a polished, trustworthy visual system suitable for legal professionals.
- Avoid a generic SaaS landing page feel.
- Avoid making the site feel like a manual lead capture form first.
- Keep the experience fast, responsive, and easy to scan.
- Use strong first-viewport product signaling.

The final page should likely include app store calls to action, concise product messaging, professional credibility cues, and a clear path to download.

## Conversion Goal

The main conversion goal is app download intent.

Primary calls to action should eventually point to:

- Apple App Store download.
- Google Play download.

Secondary conversion paths may be considered later, such as:

- Contact for partnerships.
- Professional inquiry.
- Waitlist or launch notification.

Any secondary path should stay subordinate to the app download goal unless the commercial strategy changes.

## Constraints For This Phase

This planning phase must not:

- Build the full visual landing page.
- Create a backend.
- Connect to AWS.
- Connect to Squarespace.
- Connect to GitHub.
- Connect to Unbounce.
- Deploy anything.
- Include secrets, API keys, passwords, or private credentials.
- Cancel, modify, or interfere with the existing Unbounce landing page.
