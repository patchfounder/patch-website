# Patch Landing Page Master Brief

## Project Status

This document contains the approved direction for the Patch landing page.

The website must not be built, deployed, or connected to any external service as part of creating this brief. The next phase will be the first controlled local build.

## Product Positioning

Patch is a premium speaking-coach app for lawyers.

Patch helps individual lawyers build a consistent speaking practice through structured calls with a coach, focused topic preparation, and clear performance feedback.

The website must present Patch as a premium professional product for the legal sector. It must not feel like:

- An English school.
- A language class.
- A marketplace for general language instruction.
- A student product.

## Approved Language

Use the following terminology consistently:

- Always use **coach**.
- Always describe the professional as a **coach**.
- Always use **Book a Call**.
- Never use **Book a class** or **Book a lesson**.
- The main website CTA is **Download the app**.
- Use **4 sessions to try** as supporting conversion copy.

The App Store and Google Play links are live and should be used in the future build.

## Target Audience

The primary audience is individual lawyers working at top-tier and international law firms.

Priority roles:

- Associates.
- Senior associates.
- Counsel.
- Partners.

The messaging and design should respect the audience's professional seniority, time constraints, and expectations of quality. The page should feel credible to lawyers working in demanding international environments.

## Conversion Goal

The primary conversion goal is to encourage visitors to download the Patch app.

Primary CTA:

> Download the app

Supporting conversion copy:

> 4 sessions to try

The App Store and Google Play links should be clearly available wherever the main download action appears.

## Visual Direction

The landing page should feel:

- Premium.
- Appropriate for the legal sector.
- European.
- Restrained.
- Spacious.
- Product-led.

The visual direction may be influenced by the clarity and polish of Legora, Grammarly, and Revolut, but must not copy their layouts, branding, or visual assets.

The page should prioritize:

- Strong typography.
- Clear information hierarchy.
- Generous spacing.
- High-quality product presentation.
- Restrained use of color.
- Simple, confident calls to action.
- Mobile-first usability.

Accent colour must be used sparingly. Do not use large turquoise or blue section backgrounds. The page should primarily use white, black, grey, and restrained editorial layouts, with turquoise reserved for CTAs and small product highlights.

Avoid visual conventions associated with language schools, online courses, student platforms, or general instruction marketplaces.

## Brand Colors

Use the approved Patch colors:

| Color | Hex | Intended Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Dark Turquoise | `#00A5C0` | Accent, active states, selected details, and key highlights |
| Black | `#000000` | Primary text and strong contrast |
| Grey | `#464646` | Supporting text and secondary interface details |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Primary page background and visual space |
| Orange | `#FBAD26` | Selective emphasis and supporting accent |
| Navy | `#08375D` | Premium contrast, selected sections, and product depth |

Turquoise must be used as an accent only. It must not become the dominant page background.

## Logo Direction

Use the existing PNG Patch logos for version one of the landing page.

Do not redraw or replace the logo during the first controlled build unless a later approved brief explicitly changes this direction.

## Product Journey

The page should communicate the Patch product journey in this order:

1. Download Patch.
2. Book a Call.
3. Watch the topic video.
4. Start Call with your coach.
5. Review your score.
6. Build your weekly rhythm.

This journey should feel simple, professional, and repeatable. It should show how Patch fits into a lawyer's working week rather than presenting the experience as a traditional course.

## Product Visuals

Use real Patch app screenshots as reference material for product visuals.

Do not paste raw screenshots directly into the final landing page. Recreate the relevant interfaces as cleaner, more premium product mockup cards that preserve the real product logic while improving presentation and consistency.

Important product visuals include:

- Booking flow.
- Calendar.
- Time selector.
- Topic video.
- Start Call button.
- Coach profile.
- Score dashboard.
- Average score.
- Calls left.
- Support and contact area.

The visuals should help visitors understand the app journey quickly without overwhelming them with every interface detail.

## Pricing

The approved pricing plans are:

| Plan | Frequency | Price |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Simple | 1 session per week | €48/month |
| Regular | 2 sessions per week | €88/month |
| Fast | 3 sessions per week | €120/month |

Pricing should use large, simple, mobile-friendly cards inspired by Revolut's clarity.

Pricing presentation should:

- Be easy to compare at a glance.
- Keep plan names, session frequency, and monthly price prominent.
- Avoid dense feature matrices.
- Work cleanly on small mobile screens.
- Maintain the premium, restrained visual direction.

## Recommended Homepage Structure

### 1. Navigation

Provide simple navigation, clear Patch branding, and a prominent **Download the app** action.

### 2. Hero

Position Patch immediately as a premium speaking-coach app for lawyers. Lead with the app, a clear value proposition, App Store and Google Play actions, and the supporting copy **4 sessions to try**.

### 3. Trust Strip

Establish professional credibility quickly using approved proof points, firms, roles, outcomes, or testimonial attribution where available.

### 4. Product Journey

Present the six-step journey from downloading Patch to building a weekly rhythm.

### 5. App Visual Showcase

Show refined product mockup cards based on the real app experience, including booking, preparation, calls, coaching, scoring, and account support.

### 6. Why Patch

Explain why Patch is designed for individual lawyers and how it differs from traditional language classes, generic speaking tools, and general instruction marketplaces.

### 7. Testimonials

Use approved testimonials and professional roles to reinforce relevance and trust. Do not invent testimonial quotations.

### 8. Pricing

Present the Simple, Regular, and Fast plans in large, clear, mobile-friendly cards.

### 9. Firm Enquiries

Provide a secondary path for law firms or professional organizations interested in Patch. This section must remain secondary to the individual app-download journey.

### 10. Final CTA

Repeat the primary **Download the app** action, App Store and Google Play links, and **4 sessions to try** supporting copy.

### 11. Footer

Include essential navigation, legal links, support or contact access, and Patch brand information without adding unnecessary complexity.

## Build Principles

The first controlled local build should:

- Remain a static React and Vite front end.
- Require no backend.
- Use JavaScript rather than TypeScript.
- Reuse the existing project scaffold.
- Use the existing PNG Patch logos.
- Establish the approved content hierarchy and visual system.
- Focus on responsive structure before final polish.
- Keep all work local until deployment is separately approved.

## External Services And Deployment

The first controlled local build must not:

- Deploy the website.
- Connect to AWS or AWS Amplify.
- Connect to Squarespace.
- Connect to Unbounce.
- Connect to GitHub.
- Change DNS.
- Connect to Slack.
- Connect to Linear.
- Connect to any other external service.
- Include secrets, API keys, credentials, or private data.
- Touch existing Patch app code.

All external connections, deployment work, and domain changes require separate approval.
